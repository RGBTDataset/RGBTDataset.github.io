Dataset Description

We record video pairs under sixteen different scene types,such as laboratory room, campus road, play ground and water pool, and detailedly analyze the diversity and bias of the constructed dataset in following aspects:

Object category The objects in our RGBT dataset are divided into two categories: rigid and non-rigid objects. Rigid objects, including vehicles and human heads, usually move fast and have large scale variation over time. Nonrigid objects, including humans and swans, have very high degree of freedom, and usually increases the difficulty in tracking.

Object size Small objects frequently appear in visual surveillance. Tracking of small objects is essential yet challenging. They usually are less color or structure information, and easier to lead to model drift.

Illumination condition The video sequences are recorded under different weather conditions, including sunshine, overcast sky, rainy and snow, which can bring big challenges in RGB videos. In addition, the illumination of some RGB videos significantly varies over time.

Thermal Crossover When the targets have similar temperature with other objects or background, “thermal crossover” will occur in thermal videos. In such circumstance, thermal information will be ambiguous in tracking objects.

Scale variation Scale adaptation is important to alleviate model drift. Some of our videos have large scale variation, e.g., the car is driven from far to near. We present the histogram of relative area to the first frame bounding box in Fig. 1 (d) to reflect the statistics of scale variation.

Presence of occlusion Our videos cover several aspects of occlusion, e.g., how much the target is occluded, how long the target is occluded, and the target is completely occluded in RGB (thermal) video while can be seen in thermal (RGB) video.

Moving speed We also take the motion speed of the target into account. Fig. 1 (e) shows the histogram of center distance between consecutive frames, which can reflect the statistics of moving speed.

According to above analysis, we summarize eight attributes to construct the subsets of whole dataset for facilitating evaluation of trackers on different challenging factors, as shown in Tab. 1. We also present the attribute distribution in Fig. 1 (c). Aside from presenting the performance on the whole dataset, we further analyze the attribute-based performance for better understanding the trackers under specific challenging conditions.

Table 1. List of the attributes annotated to our RGBT dataset

attribute Description
OCC Occlusion - the target is partially or fully occluded.
LSV Large Scale Variation - the ratio of the first bounding box and the current bounding box is out of the range [0.5, 1].
FM Fast Motion - the motion of the ground truth is larger than 10 pixels.
LI Low Illumination - the illumination in the target region is low.
HI High Illumination - the illumination in the target region is high.
TC Thermal Crossover - the target has similar temperature with other objects or background.
SO Small Object - the number of pixels in the ground truth bounding box is less than 400.
DEF Deformation - non-rigid object deformation
